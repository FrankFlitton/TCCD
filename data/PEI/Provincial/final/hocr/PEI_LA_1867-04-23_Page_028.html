It is thewithdrawal of the money from this Colony that makesthe pressure. I do not mean to throw any blame onthe Banks ; I know better. They must respect therights of their shareholders. If the Banks have not gotbills of exchange, they must pay ont the gold ; and, ifmeans are not obtained before the first of July, theBanks will have to close. Their object was to drive usinto Confederation. They thought it would release usfrom these difficulties, and that they money we wouldget out of Confederation would pay for the Cunardestate. They were willing to sell our rights for theself-interests of a few. But before closing, Mr. Chair-man, I would observe there is a very interesting talebeing published at London. It comes out in the peri-odicals, and is called " Birds of Prey .
