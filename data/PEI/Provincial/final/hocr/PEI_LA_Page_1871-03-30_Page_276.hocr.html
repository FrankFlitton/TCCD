Confederation was at that time the order ofthe day, and the people passed a strong re-solution against it. If they had broken thelaw we would be in Confederation to-day.The people knew that if they broke the lawthere would, in all probability, be numbersof them shot down. One of the most in-nocent men on the Princetown Road washand-cuffed and brought to jail, but no chargewhatever could be brought against him.Mr. Edmund Crabb having come out to hisown gate, was taken for a spy, pushed intoa wagon, and taken off to prison, for nocause whatever. I merely make these state-ments to show that the actions of the Gov-ernment of that day were calculated toirritate the people to that degree.
