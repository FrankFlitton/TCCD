1873 PARLIAMENTARY REPORTER. 269

THURSDAY, June 5th.

Consideration of County Courts re-sumed.

MR. LAIRD moved that the Billremain over, for further consideration,until next year.

HON. MR. POPE said that by theterms of the North America Act, theseCourts will have to be established be-fore we go into Confederation, or theDominion will not be bound to paythe Judges.

Mr. L. H. DAVIES regarded it asfoolish to establish Courts before CourtHouses were built for them to holdtheir sittings in, and thought it wouldbe wise to allow the matter to lie overuntil next session. He did not agreewith the hon. Leader of the Govern-ment that it was necessary to establishthese Courts before we went into theDominion. He would second theresolution of the hon. member theLeader of the Opposition.

After a few further remarks fromhon. members, Mr. Laird's resolutionwas put and lost.
