Hon. B. DAVIES moved that the following be added to the first para- graph of the address: " and beg to assure you of our cordial co operation in the management of public affairs."

Mr. D. LAIRD thought it neecessary to add the words proposed by his hon. colleague in order to complete the sense. His Honor asked for the co- operation of the House. but the para- graph as it stood at present, gave him no answer. This would not do ; the House should act fairly, honestly and above board in all matters and especi- ally in so important a matter as that referring to the Union of this Colony with the Dominion of Canada. His Honor asked for the co-operation of the House, and the reply as it at pre-sent stands amounts to the phrase, "goabout your business." The hon. mem-ber for Summerside (Mr. L.) statedthe other evening that they (the gov-ernment) intended to rule the country.Yes, with a rod of iron.
