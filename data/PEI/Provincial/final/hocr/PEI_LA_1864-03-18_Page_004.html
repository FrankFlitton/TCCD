FRIDAY, March 18.

DEBATE ON THE ADDRESS 1N ANSWER TO HIS EXCELLENCY’S SPEECH.    

[n the afternoon, the order of the day being read for the House in Committee of the whole on the draft Ad- dress in answer to His Excellency's Speech, it was, on motion of Mr McLennan, resolved that the House go into the order of the day. Mr John Yeo in the chair.  

  The Address was then read by the Chairman, and is as follows:—

To His Exellency George Dundas, Eesquire, Lieutenent Governor and Commander-in-Chief in and overHer Majesty's Island Prince EEdward and theTerritories thereunto belonging, Chancellor, Vice Admiral and Ordinary of the same 

The communication received by Your Excellency from the Administrator of the Government of NovaScotia, on the subject of a proposed Union of theProvinces of Nova Scotia, New Brunswick andPrince Edward Island, when laid before us, shallreceive due consideration.
