QUEEN’S PRINTER

The House then went into Committee on
Sir John A. Macdonald’s Bill for the appoint-
ment of a Queen’s Printer. Mr. Bodwell in
the chair.

The Bill was reported; third reading to-
morrow.

TELEGRAPH COMPANIES, ETC.

Hon. Mr. Langevin's Bill, respecting Tele-
graph Companies, was read a second time,
considered in Committee, reported with an
amendment, which was concurred in, and
read a third time and passed.

Sir John A. Macdonald gave notice that he
would to-morrow move the reference to Com—
mittee of the Whole of certain resolution res-
pecting Law Stamps in Ontario.

Mr. Le Vesconte moved an address for co—
pies of all correspondence with the Imperial
Government on the subject of the resolutions
of the Nova Scotia Legislature. Carried.
