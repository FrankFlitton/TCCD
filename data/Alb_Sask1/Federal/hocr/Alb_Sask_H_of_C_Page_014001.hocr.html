The policy announced in the speech of
granting provincial autonomy for the North-
west Territories marks an important epoch
in our history. It is thirty-four years ago
since the province of Manitoba was admit-
ted into confederation and given all those
rights, and that status in the federation
which is enjoyed by every province in the
Dominion, and at this moment it may be op-
portune to indulge in a retrospective glance
and recall some of the advances and de-
velopments that have occurred since con-
federation. Thirty—eight years ago when
confederation was established it embraced
but four provinces and covered a territory
stretching between Lake Superior and the
sea. Each one of these provinces was then
imbued mainly by local prejudices and limit—
ed by provincial aspirations. Each one was
