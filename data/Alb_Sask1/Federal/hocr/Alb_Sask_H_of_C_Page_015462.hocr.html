Mr. SPROULE. How will the societies
at present existing be affected ?

Mr. FITZPATRICK. They are conti-
nued in their provincial existence. They
can only be dissolved by the Governor in
Council after the province has made provi-
sion for substitutes. These societies, being
in operation, will immediately, when this
Bill passes, come under the jurisdiction of
the Governor in Council, and we must deal
with them, in so far as their present cor-
porate existence is concerned; but we will
deal with them in such a way as to dissolve
them after the province has arranged by
legislation for their continued existence
within the province. The province must

ﬁrst arrange for other societies being sub-
stituted for the present ones.
