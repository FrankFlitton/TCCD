Reg is A Tow’NSITE MATTER.

On Wednesday Mr. Walter Scott brought to the attention of the House the §º. townsite question. He briefly cited the facts of the matter and referred to the peculiar policy which characterised the actions of trustees when dealing with the Conser­vative Government in that day as compared with their attitude towards railway companies. In one case 33 acres of land were sold at $3 per acre which was afterwards testified to be worth $5 an acre. A building in Re­gina, which cost the trustees $15,000 brought $17, in rent from the Government and afterwards was sold to the Government for $16,000. So that a building which cost the trustees $15,000 was handed over to the Govern­ment for $33,000.

Mr. Scott quoted Sir Wilfrid Laurier, stating when at Regina that he held it to be very improper for a government to hold land for speculative purposes. For some years past the Government had been trying to make a division of the land with the railway company. He understood that, an agreement had been reached whereby the Government was to take one-half and the railway one-half. That being the case he asked that the Government portion of the land be alienated so that it would not be any longer exempt from taxation. He also suggested that it be given to the different municipalities. The Government had already received $180,000, while the total receipts of the trustees was about $452.000. The town of Regina should have got $56,000 in taxes and it only received $14,000. The only way to recoup the municipalities was to hand over to them the land.

Hon. Mr. Sifton in reply said that the question as to the accounts between the trustees and the Government would have to be settled in the courts. , The report of the townsite commission appointed by the Government shows *: some $30,000 had been improperly charged against the Government. The trustees disputed the findings of the commission. An arrangement had been made between the Canadian Pacific and the North-West Land ('o. and the Government by which the town lands were to be divided. He agreed with what the member for West Assiniboia, had said that the Government should not hold lands for speculative purposes, and as soon as the session was over action would be taken to alienate the Government's por­tion. As yet he could not say whether this would be done by auction or other­wise. The accounts in dispute between the trustees and the Government had been referred to the department of justice and would be settled in the courts.
