The MINISTER OF JUSTICE. Yes, the Bill is intended to provide for the constitu- tion of the Supreme Court, and also for ap- peals to the court. The intention of the amendment that my hon. friend (Mr. Scott) proposes is merely to give to the council of the North-west Territories control over the court as to the time, place and sittings of the court, as to the dividing the territories into judicial districts, as to defining the ter- ritorial jurisdiction and other provisions of that sort. They also asked that they should have control over the administration of crim- inal justice, but I was not prepared to give It to them, and they did not get it. We are responsible for the administration of crim- inal justice in these territories, and we want to keep control over its administra- tion.
