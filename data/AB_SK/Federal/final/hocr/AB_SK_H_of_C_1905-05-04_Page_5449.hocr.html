Mr. FIELDING. It must be admitted that there was no logical reason for adopting the 400,000 limit in the original Act of confeder- ation and there is no very logical reason for adopting the 800,000 in this. It is an esti- mate of the probable growth, and we know that in these Territories the growth is likely to be much more rapid than in the maritime provinces, but my own judgment is that before this arrangement has been in opera- tion a few years the whole question will have to be considered in the light of what has been said to-day, and then perhaps the sys- tem will have to be general in its applica- tion. In the meantime, with all the weak- ness of the system, what can we do to-day but conform to the principles laid down in the British North America Act ?
