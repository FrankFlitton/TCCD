I also fought on the school question in 1895 in Cardwell, and again in June 1896 was the school question fought out in my riding. From that day to this, I have remained ﬁrm and free, so far as my constituency was concerned, upon this question, and I have been thrice en- dorsed upon it. Therefore, I simply rise to announce to this House as I announced the ﬁrst time I spoke in the House and as I have previously announced to individual members, that I am, like the ex-Minister of the Interior, absolutely opposed to the education clauses as submitted in the Auto— nomy Bills ; that I will oppose them and oppose them unalterably.
