But I want to point out that until pro-
vincial powers are granted to the people
of the North-west Territories, the responsi-

Those services are much the same as the
services performed by the various pro-
vincial governments, with a few minor
exceptions. The services for which the
North-west Assembly is responsible are
the same as those of which the provincial
governments have charge. We know that
the various provincial governments have
deﬁnite and ﬁnal arrangements with the

But with regard to the North—west Terri-
tories at the present time, that responsi-
bility devolves upon this parliament, and
especially upon the Department of the In-
terior. In reality the business of governing
the North—west Territories is but a branch
of the department in the charge of the Min-
ister of the Interior. The services which the
North-west Assembly performs in the North-
west Territories are just as important and}
possibly more so than any of the services
that are performed by this federal parlia-
ment. The subjects of education and local
public works are possibly just as important
to the people up there as any of the sub-
jects with which this parliament has to
deal. As I said, the subjects of legislation

similar to those dealt with by the various
