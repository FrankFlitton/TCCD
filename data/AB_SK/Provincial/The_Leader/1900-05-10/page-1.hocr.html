NOXIOUS WEEDS.

To the Editor of THE LEADER: SIR,--In reading your report of the North-West Assembly April 12th, I noticed some very strange ideas re­garding noxious weeds and the screen­ings from elevators. Mr. Hawkes comes nearer to it than any of the members. He says that millers used the law to steal from farmers, and I might add elevators too. Have the legislative gentlemen a right to sit and legislate the farmers own away, in not allowing the screenings to be taken away, but still allow mills and elevators to sell ? It is sirwhat is taken from the elevators by the farmer that floods the country with weeds. No sir it is not, I think the Government is like the crazy man, trying to empty the tub of water with a ladle while the tap is turned on.There is a law compelling farmers to keep their farms clean, “why not enforce it,” without fear or favor to any man? Where the country gets flooded with weeds, is from those would be farmers who sit in the simall towns and do lots of talking at election times, and let out their land, also from vacant lands owned by land and railway companies. Compel every land owner to keep his lands clean, the farmers will do their share in clearing the country of the weeds, then sirthere will no need of such laws disallowing a farmer to take what is his own, and allowing the mills and elevators to have it. We send men to make laws for the greatest good to the greatest number, not the greatest goºd to a few, to the loss of many.
