The opening of the road to Peace River had been criticized. The road had been dubbed “the Yukon route.” He could say that this Government nover had any idea of building a road or any part of a road to Yukon. The road was a road to Peace River, for the benefit of of and for the opening up of the Peace River country. It was by no means a new subject. As far back as 1884 Mr. Oliver in the North-West Council had made out a strong case for the opening of the road, asking an appropriation of $2,000.
