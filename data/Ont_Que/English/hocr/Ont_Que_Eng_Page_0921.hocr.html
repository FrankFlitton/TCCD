I have always been of opinion, sincegI ﬁrst came to ponder carefully the future of Canada, thatthat future does not depend so much upon our lands as upon our waters. The land— the terra ﬁrma—of Canada is not inviting to those who have tilled the soil of Great Britain or explored the vast fertile plains to the west of Lake Michigan. Our country is just on a par with the northern part of the State of New York, and with the States of Vermont and New Hampshire in respect of climatic condi- tions and conditions of soil. But we possess one immense advantage over those countries, an advantage which gives us a distinctive position on this continent-—the possession of the noble river which ﬂows at our feet. It
