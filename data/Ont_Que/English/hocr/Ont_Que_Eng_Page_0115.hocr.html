(Hear hear, and cheers.) But, Mr. SPEAKER, I am further in favor of this scheme because it will bring to an end the sectional discord between Upper and Lower Canada. It sweeps away the boundary line between the provinces so far as regards matters common to the whole people—it places all on an equal level—and the members of the Federal Legislature will meet at last as citizens of a common country. The questions that used to excite the most hostile feelings among us have been taken away from the General Legislature, and placed under the control of the local bodies. N 0 man need hereafter be debarred from success in public life because his views, however popular in his own section, are unpo ular in the other,—for he will not have to deal with sectional ques- tions ; and the temptation to the Government of the day to make capital out of local preju— dices will be atly lessened, if not altogether at an end.
