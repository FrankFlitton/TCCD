I have said enough to shew the difference between the American federal system and that proposed for our adoption. In the American union each state is sovereign over all that immediate- ly concerns it. Here, everything would be submitted to the General Parliament. Lower Canada is opposed to free trade in money, and desires to limit the rate of interest; and yet this she could not do, inasmuch as that very ordinary question would be under the control of the General Parlianﬁnt. Whether the principle be a sound one or not, it is admitted that nine-tenths of our people desire that the rate of interest should be ﬁxed. Each state of the American union regulates questions of this kind as it chooses, without the intervention of neighboring states, or of the Washington Government. Thus, the rate of interest varies in a great many of the states, and in others it is not ﬁxed.
