Hon. gentlemen say, that this question is perfectly understood by the people of Canada, and that they are satisﬁed with the arrangement ; then what danger, 1 would ask, can there he in allowing the people a few months to consider the matter still more fully? In my opinion, it is far better to take the thing up deliberately and proceed cautiously with it, than to attempt to force a measure upon the people. so hurriedly, that they will feel hereafter, if they do not now, that you are doing them a very great injustice.
