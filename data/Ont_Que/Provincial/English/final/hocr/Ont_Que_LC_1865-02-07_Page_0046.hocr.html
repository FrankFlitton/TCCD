From this quotation it would be seen that then he was in favor of a Confederation of the several British North American Provinces, but he little thought then that within two short years such. a scheme would be submitted to Parliament. He was still in favor of Confeder- ation—(hear)—but it must be a Confederation founded on a just and equitable basis, upon principles which would be alike advantageous to all and injurious to none. If any other kind of Confederation'were agreed upon, it would contain within itself the seeds of decay and dissolution.
