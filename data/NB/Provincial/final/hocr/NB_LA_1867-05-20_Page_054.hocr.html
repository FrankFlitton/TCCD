GREAT ROADS.

Hon. Mr. McCLELAN moved theHouse into Committee on a Bill realtingto the Great Roads of the Province.

Mr. STEVENS in the Chair.

Mr. YOUNG said that last year hebrought in a Bill to have a very impor-tant road, in the County of Gloucester,placed on the Great Road list; and hewished to ask the Chief Commissionerwhy that road was not included in thisBill.

Hon. Mr. McCLELAN said that everyyear there were a large number of Billsintroduced to place certain roads uponthe Great Road list. Last year therewere seventeen, and it was evidently im-possible to comply with all. The objectof this Bill was not so much to increasethe number of miles as to place on thelist short roads leading to the Railwaysand other very public places. It will befound that he had not specially favoredhis own County in this Bill, when, per-haps it might have been supposed hewould have done so. One road did runinto the County of Albert, but it wasmore than counterbalanced by the repealof the road which leads from SalmonRiver to Point Wolf. The County ofKing's might appear to have been favor-ed, but on examination it would befound that the number of miles taken offwas more than equivalent to those nowput on the list. The short roads leading tothe Railways had been found necessaryto be placed on, while others of les im-portance had been taken off. In West-morland County there was about half amile of road leading from Moncton to thePetitcodiac Bridge, which al lwouldagree was very necessary to be placedon the last, and another short road at thePetitcodiac Station. There was oneroad in the County of Carleton, about sixmiles long, which was one of the largestadditions, and a short piece leading fromWoodstock Corner to the landing. InSAint John County he had added the roadfrom the Suspension Bridge to the Port-land Road, the road from the City to In-diantown, and about a mile and a half ofthe road from the Marsh Bridge to thePenitentiary. It would be conceeded onall hands that these were quite necessary,and he thought that partiality had notbeen known to any particular section ofthe country.

Mr. LINDSAY said that the hon. mem-ber for Gloucester need not complainthat one road had not been put on theGreat Road list, for he had applied agreat many times to have several placedon, but as yet ineffectually.

Mr. SMITH said there were severalroads in Westmorland which he wouldlike to have seen on the list, but he sup-posed it was not possible to put on all the[missing] roads, and he thought the countrynow was pretty well provided for, al-though of course he did not think theyhad received full Justice. (Laughter)

Mr. CAIE thought he had great causeof complaint, for the County of Kent wasabout the only one in which additionshad not been made. He held a petitionfrom about five hundred of the inhabi-tants of the County, praying that theroad known as the McLaughlin Roadshould be placed on the Great Road list.The matter was a very important one,for if this road were put in order the in-habitants of Welsford would be broughtvery much nearer to the Railway thanthey now are. By this road they wouldbe able to get their produce to the Rail-way by driving a distance of about thirtymiles, whereas now they ahve to goeighty, and consequently thousands ofbushels of grain which would have pass-ed over the RAilway last yaer, if the roadhad been passable, were disposed of inother ways, at a loss to the people. Whenthe Chief Commissioner was over thatway the weather was very unpleasant,and he was not able to go over the road,but he (Mr. C.) hoped the next time tohave the pleasure of driving him outthere. The road was cut through a dis-tance of twenty miles; but for want ofbridges over the streams which flow intothe Richibueto and the head waters ofthe Buctouche and Shediac Rivers, itcould not be used for the purposes re-quired. He hoped that at least nextSession the road would be put in goodorder.

The BIll was agreed to.
