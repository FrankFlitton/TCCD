QUESTION OF PRIVILEGE.

Mr. R. S. LAKE (Qu’Appelle). Mr. Speak-
er, before-the Orders of the Day are called,
. I rise to a question of privilege to call at-
tention to a Imisrepresentation of my re-
marks of last Thursday evening, which ap-
peared in the ‘Globe’ newspaper on the
31st of March. I spoke at such a late hour
that I can readily understand that some
mistakes might have crept in, and I am
quite sure the representatives of that paper
will be very glad to make the correction.
In the ‘Globe’ report I am credited with
having said that I approved of the teaching
of religious doctrine in the schools, al—
though I regretted the opportunity was not
always availed of. My actual remarks
were ‘that doctrine and dogma should be
kept to the church and to the home.’ The
report went on to say :
Although many Catholics sent their children

to the public schools, many desired to have
separate schools.

I did not express any such opinion in the
remarks -I made. .
