In 1876, Lieut.-Gov. Morris, in accordance with his sugges- tions to that effect, was requested by the Minister of the Interior, to take steps for completing the treaty, and. entrusted the duty to the Hon. Thomas Howard, and J. Lestock Reid, Esq., Domninion Land Surveyor He gave them formal in- structions, and directed them to meet the Indians together at Dog Head Point, on the lake, to treat with the Island Indians there and thence to proceed to Berens River to meet the Indians of the rapids of that river who had not been able to be present the previous year, and thereafter directed Mr. Howard to pro- ceed to the mouth of the Saskatchewan and pay the Indians the five hundred dollars for removal of the houses, and thence to go up the Saskatchewan to the Pas and deal with the Indians there, while Mr. Reid was to proceed from Berens River to Norway House, and arrange with the Indians for the removal of such of them as desired it, to Fisher’s River, on Lake Winnipeg.

These gentlemen accordingly in July, 1876, proceeded in York boats (large sail boats) to their respective destinations, and were very successful in accomplishing the work conﬁded to them.
