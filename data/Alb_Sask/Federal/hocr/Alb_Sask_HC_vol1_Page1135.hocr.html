I am quite aware that the terms of section 2'2 ot' the Manitoba Act diﬁer somewhat from the terms of section 93 of the British North America Act, and the differences are pointed out in the report of the same case, at page 270 ; but the prin- ciple is the same. As was stated by their Lordships of the Privy Council, the argu- ment thait power is conferred on the legis- lature of any province to exclusively make laws in relation to education is a fallacious one. The power conferred is not absolute, but limited. It is exercisable only—to use the words of section 93—‘ subject to the fol- lowing provisions.’ I repeat that it is neither absolute nor exclusive.
