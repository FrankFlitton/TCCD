A point which .it seems to me has hardly been accentuated as much as it might have been is this, that both the Bills and the pro- posed amendments to clause 16 go entirely beyond the Act of 1875. That Act provided for the introduction of the principle of separate schools into the Northwest, for their maintenance by the taxes of their supporters and for the freedom of these supporters from liability to taxes for maintenance of public schools; but that Act did not provide for a division of the public moneys devoted to education.
