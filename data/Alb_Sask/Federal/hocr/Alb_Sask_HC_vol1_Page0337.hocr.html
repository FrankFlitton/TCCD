What is the general scheme of the Confederation Act? The general scheme of the (‘onfederation Act, as has been pointed out by others. was to establish a national partnership between then fully independent and autonomous provinces or colonies sub- ject only to one jurisdiction. and that was the imperial jurisdiction, whatever it may have been and which I have no doubt varied from one case to the other. But the idea of the fathers of confederation was to make an agreement—a partnership agreement I will call it—for the purpose of determining the conditions, the powers and the relations of these. autonomous provinces, as between themselves and for the sake of creating a central power and enlarging the importance of their positions in British North America. They came together for the purpose of de- termining'the conditions of that partner- ship.
