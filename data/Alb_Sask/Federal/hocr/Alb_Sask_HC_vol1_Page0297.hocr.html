The separate schools of the far west will be so little separate, so little French that the teaching will be in English.

Lastly, a word as to the religious aspect of the question; it is clear that Sir Wilfrid Laurier, as a Catholic statesman, is perfectly satisﬁed of practically neutral schools for his co-religionists. Head over again attentively the discription which he makes of the so-called separate schools which exist in the west, which his Bill proposes to maintain, which our people must accept, and which the Protestants are humbly requested to tolerate, and you will see that they are really neutral or national schools, because, in the mind of Sir Wilfrid Laurier the two terms are synonymous.

Where is the separation in these schools from a religious point of view ? It does not exist more than it does from the national point of view. They are institutions which are neutral, neutral. absolutely neutral.

The famous half hour of religious teaching at the closing of each class does not change the essentially national and neutral character of the class itself. Mr. Laurier proclaims this with persistency, and he is perfectly right.
