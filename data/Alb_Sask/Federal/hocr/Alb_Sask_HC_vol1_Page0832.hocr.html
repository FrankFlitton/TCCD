But what .I want to impress upon the House is that in carving out these two new provinces in the Northwest, we should be careful to give them a clean bill of rights. We should keep hack nothing which is due to them. We should rather be inclined to be generous, and I am not aware that the ﬁnancial terms have been found fault with. But I would like the new provinces» to get more land and less cash. I would treat these provinces with gener- osity. I would give them a clean bill of rights and not withhold from them one jot or tittle of what they are entitled to. They are young provinces, just starting on their career, and are entitled to the very best send-off which the old provinces of Canada can give them. Let us send them off with a clean bill of rights. Let us give them the right to place on their statute-books any laws with regard to education which they may judge proper. Let them go out into the world with that charter of right and that freedom which belongs to the great western plains of which they are the young offspring. If we do that, we shall have no cause for regret, but if we attach a string to the right to which they are entitled. we will be creating a constant source of fric- tion. We will not be ﬁnally settling this great question, but will make of it a thorn in their side just as it has been in the side of some of the older provinces. I ask that the same mistake which was made in Mani- toba should not be made with regard to these great provinces in the west.
