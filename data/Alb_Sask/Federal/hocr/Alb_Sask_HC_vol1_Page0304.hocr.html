In the constitutions of these provinces there is not a word about education or separate schools, and therefore, according to the ar- gument of these hon. gentlemen, the logical conclusion would be that these provinces could not deal with education at all. That is where their argument would land them. Now, what does it all mean? It means simply this—and everybody of common sense can see it—that if the Dominion par- liament, in constituting a province, does not deal with the question of education, then the question can be dealt with by the province. Nobody can get away from that conclusion with the intelligent electorate of any province of this Dominion. They will say: Why did you not, as & government of the Dominion, leave that matter in the hands of these new provinces as you did all other local ques- tions, giving them provincial autonomy in every respect?
