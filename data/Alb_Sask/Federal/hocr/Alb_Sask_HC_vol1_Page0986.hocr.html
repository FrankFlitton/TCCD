Now, in this connection I may say that I believe that is a wise provision. I think that in every school all over this vast Do- million it would be a wise provision that religious instruction should be given. I am not one of those who believe in Godless schools, as they are called; I believe that among the boys and girls growing up in this Dominion of Canada to-day the great book of all books, the book that teaches a man, not only how to live, but also how to die, is almost a sealed book. They may read it at home on Sundays, but I am sorry to say that in our towns one hour a week in Sunday school is all the instruction they get; and if you leave out the opening and closing exercises of the school, in all there is not half an hour a week given to the re- ligious teaching of these children. I say that it is a lamentable thing that in this great Dominion of ours the Bible, the book we all prize and revere, is not taught to the children in these schools. Instead of ﬁnd- ing fault with that provision, I say it is one of the best provisions in the ordinances of the Northwest Territories. providing. as they have, for that half hour’s religious Instruction, and I would not object if it were double that length of time.
