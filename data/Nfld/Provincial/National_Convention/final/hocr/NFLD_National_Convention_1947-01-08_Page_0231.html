Mr. Hollett Why call it “road revenue"? It is out of place there.

Mr. Bailey For years the fishermen got a rebate on gas of 13 cents; in the Avalon Peninsula they got 11 cents. That was on Acto. We have to remember the price the Imperial Oil Co. charges — in 1929, in Newfoundland, the price was $98 for ten barrels of gasoline to the consumer; the price for the same barrels in Nova Scotia was $57; the price to the ﬁshermen, and we paid tax or duties, was $35. These are three different prices. In Bermuda they paid the same price as in New- foundland. The freight on crude oil was $2.40 a ton. Yet you could not buy a gallon of gas here under 18 cents. The Newfoundland fishermen have been bringing into the coffers of the Im- perial Oil Co. $400,000 a year on gasoline.
