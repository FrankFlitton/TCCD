NATIONAL CONVENTION

October 1946

October 30, 1946

Mr. Chairman Before proceeding to the orders
of the day, I should like to clarify a situation to
which I referred yesterday afternoon in dealing
with the motion before the Chair relative to the
sending of a delegation to Ottawa and the amend-
ment proposed by Mr. Penney, I saidI would put
the motion and then put the amendment ~— what
I meant was this; that when the debate on the
motion has terminated and when all the members
who wish to speak have spoken thereto, I shall

then read the question and then announce that to
that question an amendment has been proposed
and seconded so that you gentlemen may then
understand that you will have the right to speak
to the amendment as a new question. You quite
understand? Then when the debate on both is
over, I shall, of course, pursuant to the ordinary
practice, put the amendment first, and from that
the consideration of the motion will follow in due
course.
