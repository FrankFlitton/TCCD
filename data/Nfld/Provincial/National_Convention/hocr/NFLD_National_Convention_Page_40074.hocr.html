Mr. Chairman You have heard the motion

supported by Mr. Brown for a very hearty vote of

courtesy in coming here this afternoon, and for

wife and family

[The motion carried]

Mr. Chairman Mr. Wild, I present you with
this vote of thanks and our best wishes for your
future.

Mr. Wild I deeply appreciate your kind wishes
and I can assure you it has been a great pleasure
to me to come here and meet you. I am sorry I
have not been able to tell you more or spend
longer with you. I was in Newfoundland from
1934 to 1938 and when I was asked to come back
again in 1944, I gladly did so. I think, as Mr. Dunn
says, Newfoundland gets in your blood. I can
assure Mr. Job that when I go back I shall have
the welfare of Newfoundland in my heart and
shall be willing to do anything I can do. I hope
Newfoundland will put up a good show in the
tariff negotiations, and even though you are a
small country, the very fact that you are small is
all the more reason that you perhaps can put
something across I do hope that the deliberations
of the Convention will go forward with the same
promise shown in the last two weeks. I do wish
you the very best success in your efforts.

[ The Committee rose and reported, and the Con-
vention adjourned]
