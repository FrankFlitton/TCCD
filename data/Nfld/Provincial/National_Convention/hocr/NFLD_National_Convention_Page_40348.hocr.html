Report of the Committee on Agriculture: 1
Committee of the Whole

Mr. Butt In presenting this Agricultural Report,
I do not intend to make a speech. I hope the report
speaks for itself. There are one or two observa-
tions, however, that I would like to make. The
report is not a treatise on agriculture. It is rather
an attempt to survey the national aspects of
agriculture in Newfoundland in the round. You
will note the report is divided into five parts. One
is a slight introduction not intended to be a history
of agriculture in Newfoundland, but the idea of
the Committee as to some of the reasons why
agriculture has not taken the part in
Newfoundland’s economy that it should have.
The second part we hope is a close approximation
