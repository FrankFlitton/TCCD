Federal Taxation. The federal govem-
ment has authority under the British North
America Act to levy direct taxes, and sole
authority to levy indirect taxes. At the time
of confederation the former field of taxation
was quite unimportant, but the introduction
of the income tax in 1917 greatly increased
its importance as a source of revenue, until
today the federal government collects about
30% more revenue through direct taxation
than through indirect.
