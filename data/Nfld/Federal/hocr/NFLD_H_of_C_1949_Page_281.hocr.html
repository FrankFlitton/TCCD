Inquiries of the Ministry

Bight Hon. L. S. St. Laurent (Prime Min-
ister): The hon. member would be under a
false apprehension if he felt that nothing
could be done for the requirements of the
public service until the project was shown on
the Greber plan and approved by the national
capital planning committee. There are many
projects being considered individually and
being dealt with on that basis. The reference
was to a general report for the planning of
the national capital, looking forward for many
years. Such a general report is now being
considered by the national capital planning
committee, and, as I stated a few moments
ago, I will ask that committee to expedite its
consideration of the report so that it may be
brought before the house at the earliest
possible moment.

WHEAT BOARD ACT

AMENDMENTS or SESSION OF 1947-48—QUESTION
or NECESSITY FOR PROVINCIAL LEGISLATION

On the orders of the day:

Mr. T. J. Bentley (Swift Current): Will the
Minister of Justice inform the house whether
it is his intention to give a ruling on the need
for provincial legislation to make effective
the amendments to the wheat board act
passed during the last session, as requested
by the then premier of Manitoba?

Hon. Stuart S. Garson (Minister of Justice):
The hon. member omitted to give me any
notice of the question. I shall try to bring an
answer down tomorrow.

lations are there in this regard? I have a
great many letters—

Mr. Speaker: Order. The hon. member
knows very well that the purpose of a ques-
tion is to obtain information from the ministry,
not to give information.

Mr. Ross (St. Paul's): I could have put my
question the other way around, but it is this:
Will the minister make a statement on immi—
gration, and give the authority under which

VETERANS AFFAIRS

QUESTION AS TO SALE OF PORTIONS OF LAND
PURCHASED UNDER VETERANS LAND ACT

On the orders of the day:

Mr. G. K. Fraser (Peterborough West): I
should like to direct a question to the Minis—
ter of Veterans Affairs. Can the minister
say whether consideration has been given to
allowing veterans, who have bought land
under the Veterans Land Act, to sell a por-
tion of the two or more acres before the
ten—year period expires which is mentioned
in the agreement? If this were allowed it
would permit the veteran to pay part of his
indebtedness to the government. There are
many veterans who need permission to do
this.

Hon. Milton F. Gregg (Minister of Veterans
Affairs): I can tell the hon. member that
consideration has been given to that proposal,
but it has not been decided to put it into
effect.
