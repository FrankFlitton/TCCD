role to play in the life of our country. We
rely upon them for a large measure of the
protection which is necessary to the life of a
contented people. These separate centres of
political action in this country are the very
basis of our confederation and of the contented
and orderly life of our people.

In conclusion I therefore adjure the mem-
bers of this house to consider very carefully
their conduct before they make of the con-
