EXPERIMENTAL FARM, YUKON TERRITORY

Mr. Black (Yukon):

1. What has the establishment and operation by
the government of an experimental farm about 100
miles from Whitehorse in Yukon Territory cost.
showing items of expenditure including salaries and
wages?

2. What ﬁnancial returns have there been from
said farm?

3. What new agricultural information has been
obtained from the operation of said farm, informa-
tion not in possession of the Department of Agricul-
ture prior to the establishment of said farm?

4. What is the object of its operation?
Mr. McCubbin:

1.
Salaries ............... $ 11,379 . 19
Wages ................ 45,583 . 62
Miscellaneous ......... 19,296 . 66
Total ............. $ 76,259.47

2. Revenue received, $796.03.

3. Previous to the establishment of the
Whitehorse experimental substation, soil sur-
veys had revealed the existence of several
areas of land in the southern Yukon which
appeared suitable for agricultural production,
but no information was available on actual
crop production. The operation of this sub-
station has deﬁnitely proved that fair quality
wheat, as well as excellent quality barley and
oats, can be produced in this district, despite
the more rigorous climatic conditions as com-
pared with the Dawson area, in which investi-
gations had been previously conducted. It
has also been proved possible to produce rea-
sonably good yields of the hardy vegetables
and also of forage crops for hay and grazing
purposes.

4.

(a) To obtain information on the produc-
tion of crops and livestock, particularly in
southwestern Yukon in the region served by
the Alaska highway.

(b) To obtain by conducting co—operative
tests in other areas, such as Dawson and
Mayo, agricultural information applicable to
those districts.
