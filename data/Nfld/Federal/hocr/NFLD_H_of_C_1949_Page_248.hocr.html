hon. member for Temiscouata showed yester-

to maintain in this house the attitude I have
always held: that of stoutly defending the
rights of Quebec and the respecting of its
prerogatives and privileges.

I must add, however, that I will not vote
in favour of the amendment moved by the
leader of the opposition. I consider it to be
lacking in sincerity and like a man who would
arrange a way out so as to apply—if he should
ever become leader of the government—the
policy of centralization he advocated on Sep-
tember 8 and January 26, 1939, and which is
not likely to reassure Quebec.

Therefore I state my proposal very plainly
and clearly. If the Progressive Conservative
party will accept my amendment to the
amendment, I shall support their amendment,
but if they reject my own, I submit that
theirs will forever go down in parliamentary
history as an act of hypocrisy.
