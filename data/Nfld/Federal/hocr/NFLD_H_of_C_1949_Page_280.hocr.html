ALCOHOLIC LIOUORS

QUESTION AS T0 INQUIRY BY ROYAL
COMMISSION

On the orders of the day:

Mr. M. J. Caldwell (Rosetown-Biggar): May
I ask a question of the Prime Minister which
has some relation to the motion for papers
made by the hon. member for Broadview (Mr.
Church)? Has the government been asked to
appoint a royal commission to investigate the
liquor problem in all its aspects, but with par-
ticular reference to highway accidents? If so,
will the Prime Minister say whether such a
royal commission will be set up?

Right Hon. L. S. St. Laurent (Prime Min-
ister): To the ﬁrst part of the question the
answer is in the affirmative. To the second
part of the question the answer is that the
representations made to the committee of
cabinet have not yet been brought before
council for consideration, but that will be
done at the earliest opportunity.
