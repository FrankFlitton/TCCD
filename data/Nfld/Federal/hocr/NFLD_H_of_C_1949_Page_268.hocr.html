INDUSTRIAL RELATIONS

AMENDMENT or CHAPTER 54, STATUTES or
1947—48—DEDUCTION or UNION DUES

Mr. Arthur Williams (Ontario) moved for
leave to introduce Bill No. 19, to amend the
Industrial Relations and Disputes Investiga-
tion Act (deduction of union dues).

Some hon. Members: Explain.

Mr. Williams: The purpose of this bill is
to amend the federal labour code, which was
adopted last year, by adding a new subsection
3 to section 6 of the act. The effect of the
new subsection is to provide for the revocable
voluntary check-oft of union dues.

Motion agreed to and bill read the ﬁrst
time.

AMENDMENT or CHAPTER 54, STATUTES or
1947-48—nErmrr10N or EMPLOYEE

Mr. Stanley Knowles (Winnipeg North
Centre) moved for leave to introduce Bill
No. 21, to amend the Industrial Relations and
Disputes Investigation Act (deﬁnition of
employee).

Some hon. Members: Explain.

Mr. Knowles: This bill deletes a portion of
subparagraph (i) of subsection 1 of section 2
of the federal labour code in which employees
are deﬁned. Its purpose is to remove a dis-
crimination against certain groups of em—
ployees. In other words the bill would afford
to certain categories ot employees, now
excluded from the provisions of the act, the
right of collective bargaining.

Motion agreed to and bill read the ﬁrst
time.
