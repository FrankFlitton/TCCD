FEBRUARY 10. 1949

FAMILY ALLOWANCES

DATES or BIRTH or ADOPTED CHILDREN—FORM
LETTER 331A

On the orders of the day:

Mr. Roland Beaudry (St. James): I should
like to direct a question to the Minister of
National Health and Welfare. Is the minister
aware of the fact that the family allowances
office in Quebec has been sending out family
allowances form letter 331A to parents in the
province, asking, among other things, a ques-
tion about the dates of birth of adopted
children? If so, did the minister himself
authorize the issuance of the letter in its
present form? If not, will he not order a
revision of this form letter?

Hon. Paul Martin (Minister of National
Health and Welfare): The hon. gentleman was
kind enough to give me notice of his
question.

The form letter referred to was sent out in
all cases where dates of birth, including
those of adopted children, could not be veri-
ﬁed from provincial vital statistics records.
In such cases the experience has been that the
child’s birth had never been registered, and
it is necessary for the family allowance
ofﬁcials to request the help of the parents
themselves in furnishing information as to
the date of birth. Adopted children are of
course eligible for family allowances on the
same basis as any other children. In order
that the correct amount may be paid, and
that duplicate payments for the same children
under two different names may be avoided,
it is necessary, of course, to establish the
correct dates of birth. I may say that in all
cases where parents had advised the family
allowance ofﬁcials on their original family
allowances application form that the child
had been adopted, this form letter was not
sent out.

The purpose of the form letter was solely
to verify the date of birth of the children in
question. Since the hon. gentleman has
brought the matter to my attention I am
having the form letter looked at to make
sure whether or not in the circumstances a
revision is desirable.

GOLD

SALES FROM SOUTH AFRICA AT PREMIUM or
$6.50 PER FINE OUNCE

On the orders of the day:

Mr. Harry R. Jackman (Rosedale): I should
like to direct a question to the Minister of
Finance. In View of the approval by the
international monetary fund of the sale by
South Africa of newly—mined alloy gold to
be used only for industrial or professional
work at a premium Of $6.50 a ﬁne ounce over
the fund’s price of $35 a pure ounce, will the
government extend the same right to Cana-
dian gold producers?

Hon. Douglas Abbott (Minister of Finance):
Again I am caught by surprise by one of these
gold questions, but I am very glad to answer
my hon. friend. I have no ofﬁcial information
yet as to just what has taken place with
respect to the gold sales in South Africa. I
was informed this morning that the matter is
under consideration by the international
monetary fund, and when I have a report
as to the decision which the fund has made,
I shall be glad to communicate _it to the house.
