POTATOES—PAYMENTS IN N.B. AND P.E.I. UNDER
STARCH SUPPORT PROGRAM

Mr. Hatfield:

What was the amount per bushel or hundred-
weight paid to the starch factory in New Brunswick;
also to the starch factory in Prince Edward Island
in the year 1946-47 for potatoes processed under the
agriculture prices starch support program?

Mr. McCubbin: In the year 1946-47 potatoes
were purchased by the agricultural prices
support board from producers in New Bruns—
wick at $1.50 per barrel (165 pounds), pro-
vided the potatoes contained at least 70 per
cent Canada No. 1 grade. Such potatoes were
then sold to the starch factory at $1 per
barrel. In Prince Edward Island potatoes of
the same grade were purchased from the
growers at 92 cents per 100 pounds and sold
to the starch factory at 60 cents per 100

pounds. In all cases the potatoes sold to
starch factories were for starch making and
not for resale.
