ENEMY SHIPS CAPTURED IN WORLD WAR II—
PARTICIPATION 0F CANADIAN PERSONNEL

Mr. Fraser:

1. How many enemy ships were captured during
world war II, in the capture of which Canadian
personnel took part?

2. What was the nationality of each of such ships
captured?

3. What was the amount of cargo seized in each
case?

4. What disposition has been made to date of all
such ships and cargoes?

29087—385

Mr. Baker:

1 and 2. The total number of enemy ships
captured in world war II, in the capture of
which Canadian personnel took part, is not
deﬁnitely known, as Canadian navy and air
force personnel serving with United Kingdom
units would participate in such actions.

Two enemy ships were captured by H.M.C.
ships as follows: S.S. Weser, German mer-
chant ship, 9,472 tons gross; S.S. Capo Noli,
Italian merchant ship, 4,000 tons gross.

3. SS. Weser, $42,573.48 in value; S.S. Capo
Noli, $15,581.79 in value.

4. SS. Weser renamed Vancouver Island
under Canadian registry and operated by
Canadian government merchant marine until
torpedoed and sunk on October 15, 1941.
Cargo sold for above amount.

S.S. Capo Noli renamed Bic Island under
Canadian registry and operated by Canadian
government merchant marine until torpedoed
and sunk on October 29, 1942. Cargo sold for
above amount.
