BUSINESS OF THE HOUSE

Mr. Blackmore: What is the business for
tomorrow?

Mr. St. Laurent: The business for tomorrow
would be third reading of the bill which has
just been dealt with in committee; after that,
the resolution, which is on the order paper
for today, for an address to His Majesty to
place before the parliament of the United
Kingdom a conﬁrming statute; then the
resolution dealing with the Agricultural
Products Act, which is No. 4 on the order

paper today; then continuation of the debate
on the measure for the continuation of cer-
tain orders and regulations during the national
emergency arising out of the war, which is
No. 6 on today’s order paper.

Mr. Knowles: In that order?

Mr. St. Laurent: Not necessarily in that
order as to the last two items I have
mentioned.

At eleven o’clock the house adjourned,
without question put, pursuant to standing
order.
