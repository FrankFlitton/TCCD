This was entirely a new proposition for the
admission of Newfoundland, and the House
were entitled to discuss and ascertain the
justice or injustice of the terms on which it
was now proposed that it should enter the
Union. Nor were the terms the same. They
differed in very important and material
respects from those offered at the Quebec Con-
ference. The present aspect of the question
also differed very materially from its aspect
at that time. Then it was a question whether
