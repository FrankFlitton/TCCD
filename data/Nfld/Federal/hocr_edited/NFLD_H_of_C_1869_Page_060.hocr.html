Mr. Blake—How does the 1st clause read

The Chairman—This Act shall apply to

On the second clause, which provided for
assignments,

Mr. Blake objected to a section of it which
interfered with voluntary assignments. It was
not well, he thought, to interpose any ob-
stacle in this direction. He would rather place
restrictions on the discharge of the debtor
than raise any obstacles to his making an
assignment. In this respect he much preferred
the Bill as it originally stood.
